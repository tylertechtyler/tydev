Web analytics help determine where your online presence needs improvement

Overall web presence

We'll look at where your business stands with regards to your website, your social media presence, and your search engine presence, and make a plan together for how you can make improvements to bring in more business. If you don't have a website yet, we'll start there. If you do, I can install Google analytics on your site to give you a top down view of different metrics that can tell you a lot about how well your marketing efforts are working.

Traffic levels and sources

We use analytics tools to determine how much traffic your site is getting and what kind of devices it is coming from. We can also view reports that show us such things as the age and location of your visitors. Knowing the details of your site's vistitors on this level can give you an edge over your competition and can give an idea on how to tweak your advertising to reach the demographics you are targeting.

Acquisition reports

Acquisition reports show you where your visitors are finding out about your site:

1. Organic search - which comes from search engines like Google or Bing.
2. Direct - which is when someone types in the URL or opens a bookmark.
3. Referral - such as when a user clicks on a link on another website to get to your site.
4. Social - which is when a user is directed to your site from Facebook or another network.

Behavior reports

Behavior reports will show in detailed view of exactly what your visitors are doing on your website, which can be handy in figuring out what your visitors like and don't like. We view such things as:

1. Pageviews - which shows the total number of pages viewed by your visitors
2. Average Time on Page - which shows the average amount of time a visitor spends viewing a web page on your website.
3. Bounce Rate - this is the percentage of visitors that only view a single page and leave without interacting with it.
4. Percentage Exit - this tells you how often visitors exit your website’s page.
